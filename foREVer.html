foREVer

James Owen Sullivan, known by his stage name "The Rev," was a highly talented musician and drummer best known as the former drummer and founding member of the heavy metal band Avenged Sevenfold. Born on February 9, 1981, in Huntington Beach, California, The Rev's impact on the world of rock and metal music was profound. His distinctive drumming style combined intricate patterns, incredible speed, and a keen sense of groove, which set him apart as one of the genre's most influential drummers.

The Rev played a pivotal role in shaping the sound of Avenged Sevenfold, contributing not only his drumming skills but also his songwriting talents. His songwriting and vocal contributions can be heard on several of the band's albums, showcasing his versatility as a musician. His tragic and untimely passing on December 28, 2009, at the age of 28, left a void in the music world, and his memory continues to live on through his contributions to Avenged Sevenfold's iconic albums like "City of Evil" and "Avenged Sevenfold."

Beyond his musical prowess, The Rev was known for his charismatic and jovial personality. His presence on and off the stage endeared him to fans and fellow musicians alike, making him a beloved figure in the rock and metal community. To this day, his legacy endures, with Avenged Sevenfold and their fans honoring his memory through their music and unwavering appreciation for the incredible talent that was The Rev.

On their 5th studio album the band released a song named "So Far Away" as a tribute to thei late bandmate and childhood friend The REV. This song is still played live today as a tribute to Jimmy and his legacy.
